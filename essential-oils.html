Eucalyptus Oil or Natural Essential Oils is the generic name for distilled oil from the leaf of Eucalyptus, a genus of the plant family Myrtaceae native to Australia and cultivated worldwide. Eucalyptus oil has a history of wide application, as a pharmaceutical, antiseptic, repellent, flavouring, fragrance and industrial uses. The leaves of selected Eucalyptus species are steam distilled to extract eucalyptus oil.

Eucalyptus oils in the trade are categorized into three broad types according to their composition and main end-use:medicinal, perfumery and industrial. The most prevalent is the standard cineole-based "oil of eucalyptus", a colourless mobile liquid (yellow with age) with a penetrating, camphoraceous, woody-sweet scent.

The Clove Leaf Oil or Clove Oil or Natural Essential oils is obtained by water distillation (also, by steam distillation) from the dried buds of Eugenia caryophyllata. It is a colourless to brownish yellow liquid, having the persistent, spicy, strong and characteristic odour and taste of clove.The oil is also obtained from stem and leaves but the odour, flavour and chemical composition differ a little from the budding oil.

The clove tree is very rich in essential oil.Clove buds yield between 16 and 17 percent of essential oil, containing 85 to 89 percent of eugenol by water distillation.Clove leaves yield 2 to 3 percent of oil containing 82 to 85 percent eugenol, while the stem yields about 6 percent of oil containing 90 to 95 percent of eugenol.
